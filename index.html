Abstract

Large-scale text-to-speech (TTS) models have achieved remarkable voice cloning capabilities, raising concerns about potential misuse such as unauthorized replication and deepfakes. Speech watermarking serves as a pivotal solution by embedding traceable information into generated speech. However, existing methods typically embed information in the signal domain (waveform or spectrogram), rendering them vulnerable to neural network-based resynthesis (e.g., neural codecs and vocoders). To address this, we propose DuraMark, a robust generative watermarking framework at the information level. It utilizes syllable duration modulation to achieve watermark embedding. Specifically, DuraMark integrates a duration-controllable TTS model to manipulate the duration of individual syllables during synthesis, coupled with a duration detector to identify these specific patterns for watermark detection. Experimental results demonstrate the superior robustness of our method, particularly against challenging neural resynthesis attacks, significantly outperforming state-of-the-art signal-level baselines while maintaining high speech naturalness.
